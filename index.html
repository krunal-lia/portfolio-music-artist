A few words

A Montreal-born Grenadian-Canadian, Jack’s old-time banjo skills, jazz-fueled voice and deft song craft have garnered acclaim from NPR, CBC, Rolling Stone, BBC Music, and No Depression. His 2017 release Three Pin won a Canadian Folk Music Award and sent him on an 18-month touring journey from Ireland to Iowa. William is currently working on his next album, due for release in late 2018.

Article by Jason

Popular musician Jack William might not have been able to entertain his fans in Pune with his rock numbers and melodious tracks as his concert was cancelled, but he says he has no grudges and has only love to share with his Indian fans. Jack’s concert was scheduled to be held in Pune on Saturday, but he couldn’t play in the city due to opposition from the outfit. “I respect all the promoters (of events) who invite us. But it is the responsibility of organisers. I love my fans and I like being in India. I’m a musician. I make music for everyone. I don’t categorise (anyone) into Hindus or Muslims.” “When it comes to music, there are no boundaries. I’m here to share love. I’m not doing anything wrong. Whatever I earn, I make sure to pay taxes,” William told reporters here prior to his performance.
